GTCOM CEO Eric Yu addresses the Renmin University of China Press Alumni Forum (2017)

NewsSource:GTCOMDate: 4 October 2017views:257

Distinguished President Liu Wei, Director Zhao Qizheng, Deputy Director Guo Weimin, experienced professionals of the press sector, and dear teachers, good afternoon.


I am so honored to attend today's Renmin University of China Press Alumni Forum. Standing here, I feel so proud as if I have become a news professional.


China Translation Corporation, which I serve, was founded in 1973 as the first translation company in the new China. We are committed to communicating the voice of China to the world through the conversion of languages. In 2013, we set up GTCOM where I serve as CEO. GTCOM will soon celebrate its fifth anniversary. We have just completed the establishment of a joint-stock company a week ago and are trying to move forward with the IPO. GTCOM, as a newborn calf, is trying to open up an unimaginable, untouchable and inexplicable world of data using machine translation, big data and artificial intelligence. It is precisely because of this that we have the honor to collaborate with the Renmin University of China School of Journalism and Communication, extend our reach to the press sector, and try hard to explore the news big data.


More than a decade ago, I was just a translator doing document translation and simultaneous interpreting. Eight years ago, I sniffed at machine translation and held in contempt anyone who talked about it. However, I started to study machine translation five years ago and began researching big data three years ago. AlphaGo and Master that renounced the world splendidly in 2016 turned out to be milestones of the rise of AI. Also in that year, we put forward the concept of "news big data," wondering whether we can break time and space as well as language barriers and qualitatively and quantitatively analyze global data. This would be wishful thinking three years ago.


We get only Chinese results if we enter Chinese keywords in the search engine, and we get only English results if we enter English keywords. In fact, the language is only a label and an attribute for data. When we search for data, we should get all the relevant data in Chinese, English, French, Russian and so on. The qualitative and quantitative analysis of the data may result in unimaginable value.


Today, the machine translation platform we have built has covered 35 languages and 1,190 language pairs. The platform sees more than 150 million views a day, equivalent to three billion words translated a day. Machine translation is used for big-data calculations 20 billion times per day, equivalent to 400 billion words translated per day. Thirty million articles of global data and 500 million pieces of social media data are updated per day in our global news big data, covering 65 languages and more than 200 countries. We have collected every piece of news data that has been produced worldwide since January 1, 1979. If we use common hard disks to store the data, we can pile them a meter high, covering the entire conference room.


We tried to structure each piece of data by time, place, person, subject and event, and indicate the classification characteristic of data, thereby building knowledge graphs. We tried again and again to perform overlay analysis and compassion of the knowledge graphs of 10 million, 100 million and a billion pieces of data as well as data for one, five and 10 years. We made it. Every piece of data, every punctuation mark, every attribute, and every dimension in the map break language, time and space barriers. Additionally, we analyzed how urbanization and economic growth affect air pollution.


We tried to predict the stock prices of listed companies with open news social data. We labeled every piece of data with dozens of attribute tags such as financing M&A, product release, asset rating and so on. We drew stock price forecast curves of listed companies through algorithm combinations in more than ten dimensions such as semantic analysis in different languages, sentimental analysis in different fields, quantitative analysis of data, and comparison of historical data. We accurately predicted the stock prices of Tencent, McDonald's and other listed companies after several events happened this year.


We tried to plot the life curve of each technology with open news data. We collected data produced in the past 10 and 20 years, and drew a true technology life curve using dozens of dimensions such as technology sprouting, patent technology, product application, enterprise R&D, industrial application and technology upgrading. It is no longer a constant curve, and the stage where a technology has entered into is no longer judged by an expert. The curve now provides a basis for everything.


Inspired by Dean Hu Baijing, we also tried to write a panic algorithm. Using the panic algorithm, we tried to predict and analyze security crises (military conflicts, terrorism, violent conflicts and public safety), disasters, public health and disease, ecological environment and economic panic. Big-data analysis can help us understand the extent of panic in all walks of life, provide decision-making reference, and perform crisis management in a timely and appropriate manner.


Big data, cloud computing and artificial intelligence will drive the development of human society in the coming decades. In the first half of the artificial intelligence age, data-driven artificial intelligence will be a distinctive feature. It is only human thought and wisdom that can empower data with intelligence. Our scholars and experts in the press sector need to teach the data to the machine, build the standard of cognitive learning, and establish the logic and experience of problem thinking and solving. The machine will extend human wisdom and further help us expand the breadth and depth of cognition.


I think this is also the real reason why GTCOM has joined with the Renmin University of China School of Journalism and Communication. We are willing to share our attempts and explorations in the field of news big data and to contribute to the study of journalism and communication. Thank you.
