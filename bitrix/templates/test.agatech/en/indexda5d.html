Integration of overseas scientific research resources and commercial channels: GTCOM accelerates its layout in Europe

NewsSource:GTCOMDate: 6 November 2017views:289

To expedite the advancement of the company's big-data business in the European market, from October 30 to November 3, Eric Yu, CEO of Global Tone Communication Technology Co., Ltd. (GTCOM), led a team on a trip to London and Vienna, where they conducted intensive and in-depth communications with Taylor & Francis Group (T&F), Informa Group (Informa), Deloitte UK (Deloitte), Newsweek London, the University of Bristol, and the Austrian Research Institute for Artificial Intelligence (OFAI) on cooperation in fields such as artificial intelligence, big data and machine translation. The results were very gratifying.

Founded in 1798, T&F is one of the largest academic publishing groups in the world with more than 2,000 employees worldwide. On October 30, Yu Yang was invited to visit the headquarters of T&F, where he had in-depth discussions with the members of the T&F Board of Directors concerning the intent of big-data cooperation. The respective parties will integrate respective superior resources for strategic cooperation and layout in the AI big-data market.


T&F


GTCOM discusses with all T&F board members


On October 31, GTCOM was invited to visit Informa, the parent company of T&F, and conducted in-depth discussions with Informa's Board of Directors. Informa is one of the Financial Times Stock Exchange (FTSE) 100 Index enterprises and one of the world's leading providers of information, knowledge and conventions, with operations in at least 40 countries and more than 7,500 employees. Through communications, both sides will conduct in-depth strategic cooperation in fields such as consultation, finance and healthcare.


Informa, parent company of T&F


GTCOM communicates with Informa board members


On November 1, Eric Yuheld talks with the management team of Newsweek London as well as the Corporate Finance M&A partners and a team from the headquarters of London-based Deloitte UK.

Newsweek is a weekly magazine that has won the highest number of honors for its excellent comments among U.S. magazines devoted to politics. In fact, Newsweek, Time and U.S. News & World Report are known as the top three news weekly magazines. The two sides discussed cooperation in the fields of news big data and financial big data. That same day, Eric Yu and his colleagues visited the London headquarters of Deloitte UK, and the two sides will conduct strategic cooperation and coordination on the application of big data to consulting services as well as overseas mergers and acquisitions.


Newsweek


GTCOM communicates with Newsweek


Deloitte Touche Tohmatsu Limited


GTCOM visits the London headquarters of Deloitte UK


Eric Yu and his colleagues were invited to visit the University of Bristol on November 2, where they communicated with the professors and scholars from the Faculty of Engineering, the School of Modern Languages, and the Big Data Research Institute. Founded in 1876, the University of Bristol is one of the top 10 comprehensive universities in the U.K. and has fostered a total of 12 Nobel Laureates to date. Winston Churchill, former Prime Minister of the U.K., served as the university's superintendent for 36 years. During the visit, Eric Yu, on behalf of the Language Big Data Alliance (LBDA), signed with the School of Modern Languages concerning its entry into the LBDA. Additionally, GTCOM reached comprehensive cooperation with the Big Data Research Institute concerning joint research on big-data technology and personnel training.


University of Bristol


Communicating with professors and scholars of the University of Bristol



The School of Modern Languages joins the LBDA


Eric Yu and his colleagues held talks with Robert Trappl, Director of the Austrian Research Institute for Artificial Intelligence (OFAI), on November 3. In 1984, Professor Trappl founded OFAI, the world's first artificial-intelligence research institute. He himself is a top scientist in many fields such as global cybernetics, artificial intelligence and psychology. He took on several important positions, such as Head of the Institute of Medical Cybernetics and Artificial Intelligence, Medical University of Vienna, and Chairman of the Austrian Research Institute for Control. He was hired as an expert in artificial intelligence by several European governments and internationally renowned companies. As a TED speaker, he delivered a speech on artificial intelligence in 2011. Professor Robert Trappl has been studying artificial intelligence for nearly half a century, and has published more than 180 papers and 34 books in leading academic journals such as Springer and MIT Press. At the meeting with Professor Robert Trappl, GTCOM reached an intention of cooperation on the entry of the OFLA to the LBDA as well as his service as an expert consultant to the 2020 Cognitive Intelligence Research Institute.


Austrian Research Institute for Artificial Intelligence (OFAI)


GTCOM visits the Austrian Research Institute for Artificial Intelligence


The visit marks GTCOM's effort to further accelerate the introduction of global artificial intelligence research resources and achieve the overall layout of the big-data business in Europe.
