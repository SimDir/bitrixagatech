China Translation Research Center Publishes Report on Global Development Trend of Language Service Industry

NewsSource:GTCOMDate: 26 August 2014views:230

On August 25, in the presence of more than 40 sinologists, translators, educators, diplomats and nearly 100 other guests, Lin Guofu, Secretary General of China Translation Research Center and General Manager of China Translation & Publishing Corporation, announced the official establishment of China Translation Research Center. At the ceremony, China Translation Research Center issued the Blue Book of Language Development Trends - Report on the Development Trend of Global Language Service Industry.


China Translation Research Center is committed to creating an international platform for communication and cooperation in the field of cultural communication and translation, and promoting the diversified and healthy development of the translation and publishing industries.” said Eric Yu, Executive Deputy Secretary General of China Translation Research Center and Deputy General Manager of China Translation & Publishing Corporation, “bearing such a mission in mind, China Translation Research Center will carry upon its shoulders important tasks such as language research, translation services, as well as the general development trends and relevant issues in the cultural, publishing and communication industries. ”


According to Yu, the language service industry is currently growing rapidly and we can see that cloud computing, the Internet, and big data are exerting growing influence on all aspects of our life. And they are also exerting influence on the language service industry. So it is time for the industry to change and a revolution is in progress. The industry is embracing the Internet and big data. Information technology is merging with natural languages.


Yu disclosed some details of the report at the release ceremony, saying that the development trend of the global language service industry in the future can be described in three parts, future demand for language services, future organizational form of the industry, and the future structure of the language service industry. Ten major trends are predicted in the report, including cross-language information acquisition and communication with no barrier, a virtual organizational structure of language services, growing popularity of crowdsourcing, language services on the cloud, streamlined service chain, and more.



Trend 1：Larger total demand, higher frequency, more individual customers

For both organizations and individuals, there will be more frequent cross-regional activities and cross-language communication. Communication occurs more and more in real time propelled by the general trend of automation, systematization, and informatization. So, language services must be more and more responsive.

As the cost of transportation and telecommunication lowers, individual customers will have higher demand for generic language services besides improving their own language abilities.



Trend 2 ：More scattered customers, more extensive sources, and demand with a tiered structure

Globalization pushes forward the process of economic integration. Customers will be scattered in a broader scope, and language services will be more easily available.

Language service providers will need to operate across different continents.

Global events, transnational communication, and individual needs, language services will penetrate into all aspects of our life and every corner of society.



Trend 3 ：Cross-language information acquisition and dissemination will see no barrier

One may acquire all information of the world and the whole world can hear the voice of one single person. No matter which language one speaks, he will be able to take part in communication without any barrier for new language services will be real-time, interactive, and all-embracing.



Trend 4 ：Virtual organizational structure and higher popularity of crowdsourcing

Information technology will boost the automation level of the language service delivery process, reduce human interference, enhance the responsiveness and efficiency of language services, and make the services more standardized.

Language service providers will gradually move away from the physical world and turn into virtual organizations. Language services will be delivered fully online. Collaboration between in-house translators will gradually be replaced by crowdsourcing and translator will be more closely connected.



Trend 5 ：Language services will be available anytime, anywhere

The scenarios of language services will be more diverse and flexible, meeting every fragment of demand, with services mainly delivered via mobile Internet.

Language services will be integrated into all kinds of telecommunication, social networking, and comprehensive service applications and some services may even run totally on the backstage.



Trend 6 ：Language services on the cloud with a streamlined service chain

Users will no longer face a number of language service providers. They will instead face a number of solutions. They will be connected to language services on the cloud and translators will respond to their demand without much exchanges.



Trend 7 ：Integrated language services will be the driving force for the globalization of other industrial platforms

Globalization gives rise to demand for language services in all kinds of industries for companies will no longer serve only customers in their own region, but also global users. So they need better integrated language services to provide better services to their customers. So language service providers will be seen more and more in cross-industrial integration projects.



Trend 8： Free-of-charge Internet gives birth to new business models in the language service industry

Language service will be the fourth largest demand of netizens, following search, social networking and online shopping. Following the Internet-based way of thinking and relevant technologies, language services will be provided for free and new business models will come into being.

Payment will be required at a different part of the service chain. More basic language services for the general public will be provided for free and industry-specific language services will accept Internet-based payment.



Trend 9 ：Precise match of supply demand of language services will boost efficiency of the industry

The mainstream of language services in the future will be specialized, customized, multi-language and multi-tiered. Language service providers on cloud platforms can reach out to a large number of language service professionals, match them with the demand more precisely through crowdsourcing, follow integrated and automated service processes, share resources, advocate collaboration between more specialized service providers, and enhance the standardization level to boost the overall efficiency of the industry.



Trend 10 ：internet technology and cloud platforms will gather resources of the industry and give birth to monopoly in the industry

Globalization pushes up the overall scale of the language service industry and M&A is seen more frequently in the industry, pushing the industry towards integration.

Riding the tide of the Internet, language service providers who have advanced technologies will become buyers of other companies and as they grow bigger, they will turn to a virtual organizational structure to realize global presence and provide services to global customers via cloud platforms.


In the end, Yu also said that the ten major trends are not cursors for the future of the language service industry; they will be verified and modified as information technology merges with natural languages.

Whoever embraces the Internet fully will probably lead the revolution of the language service industry. He is confident in a bright future of the industry.
