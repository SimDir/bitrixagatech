GTCOM Becomes Language Service Provider of the 17th Shanghai International Film Festival

NewsSource:GTCOMDate: 14 June 2014views:230

On June 14, 2014, the 17th Shanghai International Film Festival opened as scheduled. It is the largest and most influential Class-A international film event in the Asia Pacific. The event received 1,099 films for the competition and 1,808 for the exhibition from 102 countries, setting new records. As a leading language service provider in China, Global Tone Communication Technology Co., Ltd. (GTCOM) provided language services to the event just like it did last year.



Shanghai International Film Festival aims to carry culture forward, promote cultural exchanges, build an all-embracing market, and support new film makers. After more than 20 years since its inception, it has become one of the most important film festivals in Asia and a major platform for film-themed exchanges between China and the world. Today, in the new era, Shanghai International Film Festival is building a new platform for the development of China's film-making industry and making its contributions to international communication in the film industry and the Going Global campaign of the Chinese film industry.


Before it closes on the 22nd, more than 300 selected films from around the world will be showed within nine days, involving hundreds of different languages and the demand for language services will be huge. Also, as a top-level film event in China, the event poses relatively high requirements for translation and interpretation services and a language service provider must be highly capable to meet them. GTCOM built on the experience it gained in serving the previous version of this event last year and created the most professional team to provide customized translation and interpretation services, helping people communicate, building ties between the films and cultures of different countries, fully unleashing the value of cultural exchanges, and making its contribution to the Going Global campaign of China's film industry.


Language is one of the most important factors that determine whether a film can be accepted out of its home country. Subtitle translation traditionally requires a large quantity of human and material resources and takes quite a long time to complete. To lower the cost and push up efficiency, GTCOM created YeeCaption. It completes the timing, fills in the original texts and does the translation promptly. One can apply the results of machine recognition by simply selecting a function and follow the instructions to click "next" for several times to reach the final step of human editing. Users no longer need to go through complex processes to produce subtitles but can still enjoy a film just like some professionals had worked on the subtitles.


Subtitle translation is only part of GTCOM's language services. The company is dedicated to providing language services powered by innovative technologies and helping Chinese culture go global. GTCOM, a controlled subsidiary of China Translation and Publishing Corporation, the exclusive language and training service provider of the United Nations, carries forward the experience accumulated by its parent company in the past 40 years in the industry and has succeeded in providing language services to many major international events like the 2008 Beijing Olympic Games and the Shanghai Expo 2010. Now, GTCOM provides real-time language services for all kinds of institutions and individuals from around the world with YeeCloudTM, the first smart language cloud in the world. Users may connect to YeeCloudTM from any terminal at any time to enjoy smart, integrated, customized and open language services online.


GTCOM will continue to deliver quality services to the film festival with its highly capable team, so as to help with the promotion of more outstanding films and the brand building of Shanghai International Film Festival.


[About CTPC]

China Translation & Publishing Corporation (CTPC) was established in March 1973 as the United Nations Document Group. In April 1976, it was renamed Beijing Translation and Publishing Office as approved by the State Council. Then, in August 1979, it got the name China Translation and Publishing Corporation, again as approved by the State Council. CTPC has successively been put under the administration of the State Publishing Administration, General Administration of Press and Publication, and China Publishing Group. It is an important member of Translators Association of China.



[About GTCOM]

Global Tone Communication Technology Co., Ltd. (GTCOM) is a controlled subsidiary of China Translation & Publishing Corporation (CTPC). Carrying forward the high service quality which is a tradition of CTPC over its history of 40 years, GTCOM delivered services to the Olympics Games held in Beijing in 2008, the Shanghai World Expo of 2010, the Asian Youth Games in Nanjing and various other major national-level events. Also, it has been providing language services to the United Nations and many other organizations and individuals for years as a national-level language service provider.


GTCOM pools advantageous resources from China Publishing Group Corp. and China Translation & Publishing Corporation to build YeeCloudTM with world-leading speech information technology and Internet and mobile Internet technologies. YeeCloudTM includes three major platforms: the multilingual cloud call center and video conference platform, the multilingual information analysis and management platform, and the cloud language education platform; and two big data resource banks: the cloud language resource database and the global translation expert database. Adopting new service delivery models such as multilingual cloud call center and video conference, online delivery, mobile Internet terminal application, etc., YeeCloud allows global users to get connected via any terminal from any place and at any time and enjoy smart, integrated, customized and open cloud language services.
