A pioneering view: How to drive industry transformation with data

NewsSource:GTCOMDate: 21 December 2017views:274

Global Tone Communication Technology Co., Ltd. (GTCOM) successfully held the "NexMagic" annual summit on December 20, 2017. With focus on data and wisdom, the experts and scholars in the fields of technology, finance, and journalism discussed the future prospect of the big data industry, how innovative technologies such as big data and artificial intelligence can promote industrial transformation, as well as the resulting changes and coping methods.


Big data + AI reshaping news production and dissemination process


At the forum themed "News Big Data Leading the Intelligent Media Era," five industry leaders, including Wu Chenguang, the editor-in-chief of Yidianzixun.com; Yu Guoming, the director of the School of Journalism and Communication of BNU; Xu Bin, the deputy director of Tsinghua University Global Innovation Exchange; Shen Hao, a professor from Communication University of China; and Shan Xuegang, the deputy director of the People's Daily Online Public Opinion Data Center and deputy general manager of People Online (peopleyun.cn), held in-depth discussion regarding the changes brought forth by technological progress in the news industry.

News dissemination has shifted from the default "planned economy model" to the "market economy model" that is automatically recommended based on user needs. The advent of artificial intelligence, however, has disrupted the long-practiced methodology of news production and dissemination. Therefore, according to Wu Chenguang, algorithms must understand and/or accommodate human nature. In the future, the news-production process will be more automated, and consequently sentient, considerate artificial intelligence will be the technology that can serve mankind. Yu Guoming added that the "gatekeepers" in traditional news are being replaced by artificial intelligence but that currently the content provided by AI is in great need of quality improvement. Technicians should put people first and teach AI to accommodate people's needs so that society can benefit from accurate, readable news reporting.

The guests expressed their opinions on whether AI technology will replace journalists. According to Shan Xuegang, the evolution of future technologies will replace most of the news practitioners, but it will be difficult to replace senior news practitioners who have unique insights and personalized writing styles. In the opinion of Shen Hao, big-data technology has built a huge global news database. Accordingly, the journalists of tomorrow should embrace technology and use databases as the means to satisfy more demanding work requirements.

Xu Bin asserted that the impact of AI on the news industry not only stays at the individual level but is also evident in the decision-making of corporations and governments. If the technology of news knowledge graphing is introduced in the field of crisis public relations, it will be able to detect the crisis in advance and prevent it.

The development of artificial intelligence should be people-centered

Human beings are ushering in an era of "connectivity" led and driven by AI. Thus the question of how to make the best use of artificial intelligence will have a profound impact on society and daily life. At the frontier forum held under the banner "Key Technology and Value Exploration of Artificial Intelligence," Zhao Tiejun, the director of the 2020 Cognitive Intelligence Research Institute; Lian Jing, the CEO of PereDoc, Inc.; Qian Li, the director of the Information System and Knowledge Computing Center, National Science Library, Chinese Academy of Sciences; Ma Xiujun, the deputy director of the Peking University Information Technology Institute; and Professor Xiong Deyi, from the School of Computer Science & Technology, Soochow University, discussed the application of technology and the value of information mining made possible by artificial intelligence.

Qian Li noted that artificial intelligence can only become smarter and do more for humans if continuous improvement is made with respect to cloud and big-data technology. Ma Xiujun said that leading-edge intelligence technology will create an ocean-current environment for the flood of big data and nurture an ecosystem of emerging smart industries. Xiong Deyi then expressed his opinion on the future trend of artificial intelligence, asserting that AI cannot be separated from language intelligence. Language, he said, is a unique human ability, so only artificial intelligence that can see and speak can better serve humankind.

Artificial intelligence of global concern has also had a profound effect on many vertical areas. At the forum, Lian Jing, as a representative of the big data vertical area practitioner, expressed her opinion on how to use the big data and artificial intelligence to mine the value behind the massive data of the medical industry and better serve the health of the Chinese people. With regard to the future development, Zhao Tiejun was optimistic that the synergy of big data, artificial intelligence, mobile computing, cloud computing, and the Internet of Things will enable the rapid development of artificial intelligence. Accordingly, in 2020 there will be exponential growth in the market for artificial intelligence, and that growth will be extremely significant to China.


Promoting the sound development of the financial industry with big-data technology


The financial industry, driven by the technological revolution, has ushered in a third great leap. At the forum held with the theme "Cross-Industry Integration and Innovative Development of Financial Big Data," Zhu Keli, an expert from the Development Research Center of the State Council and founder of SmartStone ThinkTank; Wang Zhen, cofounder of Beijing Caigin Information Technology Co., Ltd.; Du Xiaojun, cofounder and general manager of Runup (Group) Co., Ltd.; and Yang Xiayun, the vice president and CTO of Jiuyi Hengyuan (Beijing) Technology Co., Ltd. discussed the ways in which big-data technology can promote further development in the financial industry.

Wang Zhen, speaking in regard to the application of big-data technology in the financial industry, asserted that big data has a significant role in the fields of credit, data-investment research, smart customer service and intelligent investment consulting, adding that such applications are very productive and profitable. Du Ziaojun used a simple but powerful metaphor to describe it: "If finance isn't supported by big data," he said, "innovation is like water without a source."

Big-data technology provides new solutions to risk control in the financial industry. Zhu Keli believed that financial practitioners should not only focus on data collection but must also continue to enhance their professional capabilities while analyzing and restoring the truth behind the massive data. Only in this way, he said, can we further improve the risk control of financial institutions. Yang Xiayun said asserted the essential aspects of quality and connectivity in data, stating that the connectivity of government data, online transaction data and social data makes it possible to manage and control financial risks more effectively.
