Chinese Key Words and International Cultural Communication Session Concludes in Beijing

Organized by China Translation Research Center, the Chinese Key Words and International Cultural Communication Session was held in Beijing on August 24, 2015, as part of the 2nd International Forum on Cultural Communication. Present at the session were Li Yan, Deputy General Manager of China Publishing & Media Holdings Co. Ltd.; Huang Song, Party Secretary of China Translation Corporation, more than 40 senior diplomats, renowned sinologists, and senior translators, as well as guests from the diplomatic, translation, and international communication industries.


Li Yan, Deputy General Manager of China Publishing & Media Holdings Co., Ltd.,

giving his speech


China Translation Research Center hired 15 new advisors, including Ms. Shi Yanhua, senior diplomat and former ambassador to Belgium; and Mr. Chen Mingming, former ambassador to New Zealand and Sweden and senior expert of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs. Certificates were granted to them during the session. With these experts on board, China Translation Research Center now has an expert panel that covers a wider range of fields and has higher academic attainment.


Granting certificates to the experts


Another highlight of the event was the launch of China Key Words and its official website (www.cnkeywords.net), revealing the background and content, as well as the technologies behind them. China Key Words covers 12 categories including politics, economy, science and technology, and culture, and incorporates one million entries in 17 languages. It gives an authoritative presentation of China to the outside world in a brand new perspective and disseminates China's voice worldwide. China Publishing Group and China Translation Research Center gather the wisdom of senior translators and sinologists from around the world and make use of the most advanced big data and semantic analysis and search technologies to present more systematically how the key words of China came into being over time. Global users may look through core words along a timeline to feel the dynamics of China in a more vivid way.


Official website of China Key Words

In the discussion during the session, Ms. Shi Yanhua and Mr. Chen Mingming, senior experts of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs; Ms. Liu Heping, President of the School of Translation and Interpretation of Beijing Language and Culture University; and Ms. Cheng Wei, President of the School of Interpreting and Translation of Beijing International Studies University shared their opinions on the definition and coverage of Chinese key words, effective ways of international communication, and tips of relevant translation, giving inspirations to the audience. Foreign guests such as Giray Fidan, famous sinologist from Turkey; B. R. Deepak, sinologist from India; and Tyler Roney, Executive Editor-in-Chief of The World of Chinese, shared their insights in what role China Key Words may play in cultural communication and translation and what is more valuable to foreigners who want to know China better. Through such discussions, experts offered their suggestions concerning how to better build China Key Words and how to disseminate the Chinese culture more effectively. The event was fruitful.


Chinese speakers


Foreign speakers


Following the first version of the forum held in 2014, China Publishing Group gathered renowned sinologists, translators, and authors from around the world for one more time at this year's forum. The purpose was to build ties between these people and outstanding publishers and copyright agencies and let them have exchanges and contribute more to the going global campaign of Chinese culture. The Chinese Key Words and International Communication Session was part of the 2nd International Communication Forum on Chinese Culture. It offered important insights for the international communication of the Chinese culture.


Group photo of experts


According to an expert of China Translation Research Center, in the new era, we should take a new perspective to share and communicate China's development and the Chinese culture, and in this process, a major concern is how to make full use of the Internet/mobile Internet and big data technology to help with the communication of the Chinese culture.

For more information about China Translation Research Center, please follow its WeChat account. Visit the official website of China Key Words at http://www.cnkeywords.net.
