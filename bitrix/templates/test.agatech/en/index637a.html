China's First Big Data News Lab Established, Leading the Industry towards a New Layout

NewsSource:GTCOMDate: 31 October 2016views:245

On October 29, 2016, the First China Public Opinion Annual Meeting was held in Guangdong University of Foreign Studies (GDUFS). Global Tone Communication Technology Co., Ltd. (GTCOM) and GDUFS joined hands to launch the joint lab of big data news and international public opinion monitoring and release, the first of its kind in China. The two sides will launch strategic cooperation in the theory and practice of international journalism and communication, talent cultivation, government communication and crisis management, and other fields, with an aim to build the lab into a high-end think tank with global influence, which conducts public opinion monitoring and release, as well as consulting for decision makers.


The agreement was signed by Shi Youqi, Deputy Party Secretary of GDUFS and Wang Xiaodong, Assistant General Manager of GTCOM and General Manager of GTCOM Qingdao, on behalf of the two sides in the presence of Sui Guangjun, Party Secretary of GDUFS; Eric Yu, CEO of GTCOM; Chen Changfeng, President of the Journalism History Association of China and Executive Vice President of the School of Journalism and Communication, Tsinghua University; Yang Kui, President of the GDUFS School of Journalism and Communication; and Prof. Chen Lidan of the School of Journalism and Communication, Renmin University of China.


GTCOM and GDUFS signing the agreement to launch the joint lab of big data news and international public opinion monitoring and release


In addition, GTCOM CEO Eric Yu delivered a speech titled "news big data in the time of AI". From the perspective of big data and AI, he described the large amount and the specific portrait of news data out there on the global Internet, mobile Internet, social media, and new media. Then he introduced to participants the huge value that cross-language big data analysis of YeeSight may bring to public opinion research worldwide, taking natural disaster and violent terrorist attack as examples.


Eric Yu delivering his speech


The first news big data lab of China launched to promote international public opinion research and practice in China


One of the two sides of the cooperation, GDUFS, has a key research base at the university level, i.e. the government communication and international public opinion research center. The other side, GTCOM, boasts high language capacities, big data analysis capacities, and cognitive intelligence technologies. They will carry out in-depth cooperation in regard to the research and practice for the cultivation of talents for international journalism and communication, government press release, government crisis PR and emergency response, and region and city image building in the background of globalization and full media.


According to Yang Kui, President of the GDUFS School of Journalism and Communication, this joint lab will help China's news and public opinion industry build strong and comprehensive capacities for news database building and data analysis and lay a solid foundation for the theoretical and practical research of international public opinion. In the meantime, against the background of the Belt and Road Initiative, it intends to build a new-type high-end think tank platform that gathers government bodies, media agencies, enterprises, higher education institutions and research institutes for full media development, scientific research, talent cultivation, and social services to contribute to the implementation of the Belt and Road Initiative and achieve the goal of "pursuing the Chinese dream with local wisdom and listening to voices worldwide with an international perspective", so as to promote the research on international public opinion in China and the development of the discipline.


The amount of the cross-language big data grows exponentially and analysis capacity brings limitless potential value to the press industry


Today, the overall media environment has changed tremendously. As mobile Internet develops, massive data is generated through all kinds of media: newspaper, television, web portals, and SNS like Weibo, WeChat, Facebook, and Twitter. Cross-language big data is on a magnitude far beyond our current understanding. It contains all the information about all countries and regions in the world, at all times, and concerning all people, events, and things. And it grows exponentially. GTCOM is dedicated to breaking the boundaries of data and upgrading the way in which we acquire data analysis capacity, so as to bring to every enterprise or even every individual person strong big data analysis capacities and cognitive power that we have never seen before. This will bring immeasurable value to the journalism and communication industry in China and even the world by benefiting news production, public opinion prediction and communication, and the evaluation of communication effectiveness.


Work more on industrial application to speed up the globalization of GTCOM in the big data industry


Since the launch of YeeSight, the world's first cross-language big data product, on July 30, GTCOM has joined hands with Guangdong University of Foreign Studies and Macao Polytechnic Institute to establish the joint lab for Chinese-Portuguese-English machine translation and signed a strategic cooperation agreement with Alibaba Cloud at the Computing Conference, launching a number of cooperation projects on machine translation, big data and artificial intelligence. As cognitive intelligence technologies mature, language capacities, big data analysis capacities, and cognitive intelligence capacities hold out a bright future for GTCOM. GTCOM wishes to create complete knowledge maps for global users in the fields of government affairs, Internet, journalism, sports, medical services, finance, etc. so as to help the Chinese government and enterprises go global, make it easier for the world to have a better understanding of China, lead foreign companies into China, and speed up the march toward a borderless cyber world.
